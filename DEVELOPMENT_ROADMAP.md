# Nowercise Development Roadmap

## Vision
Transform Nowercise from a good exercise app into the definitive cancer-recovery exercise platform by adding features that address the unique psychological, physical, and clinical needs of cancer patients.

---

## Phase 1: Psychological Safety (Week 1-2)
**Goal:** Build patient confidence and remove fear of movement

### 1.1 Confidence to Move Score ✅ IN PROGRESS
- Weekly confidence tracking widget on dashboard
- Trend chart showing progress over time
- Prompts: "I feel safe moving today", "I trust my body"
- **Impact:** High - addresses #1 barrier to exercise in cancer patients

### 1.2 "I Have 3 Minutes" Button ✅ IN PROGRESS
- Prominent fatigue-day option on dashboard
- Micro-workout options: breathing, seated mobility, circulation
- No guilt, no planning required
- **Impact:** High - maintains habit on worst days

### 1.3 Red-Amber-Green Signals ✅ IN PROGRESS
- Color-coded safety guidance when logging symptoms
- Clear action steps for each level
- Links to educational content
- **Impact:** High - empowers safe self-management

---

## Phase 2: Treatment Intelligence (Week 3-4)
**Goal:** Make the AI truly cancer-native

### 2.1 "What to Expect This Week" Panel
- Treatment-aware guidance based on chemo/radiation cycles
- Expected symptoms and energy levels
- Movement recommendations adapted to treatment phase
- **Impact:** High - reduces anxiety and improves adherence

### 2.2 Treatment Phase Modulation
- Auto-adjust workout intensity around treatment days
- Explicit messaging: "This week is lighter - that's intentional"
- Recovery windows built into scheduling
- **Impact:** Medium - prevents overexertion

### 2.3 Oncology Appointment Summary
- One-tap 4-week summary for medical appointments
- PDF export for doctors
- Clinical language toggle
- **Impact:** High - builds trust with medical team

---

## Phase 3: Community & Support (Week 5-6)
**Goal:** Reduce isolation while avoiding toxic positivity

### 3.1 "Same Boat" Peer Matching
- Match by cancer type, treatment stage, age bracket
- Focus on companionship over competition
- Optional feature - not forced
- **Impact:** Medium - reduces isolation

### 3.2 Quiet Wins Feed
- Celebrate realistic achievements
- "Walked to the mailbox", "Moved despite fear"
- No pressure, no comparison
- **Impact:** Medium - positive reinforcement

### 3.3 Intervention Triggers
- Auto-flag patients for specialist attention
- Missed streaks, rising pain, falling confidence
- Proactive support before dropout
- **Impact:** High - retention and safety

---

## Phase 4: Advanced Safety (Week 7-8)
**Goal:** Specialized features for high-risk conditions

### 4.1 Lymphoedema-Aware Layer
- Limb-specific profiling
- Avoid rapid volume spikes in affected areas
- Early warning pattern detection
- **Impact:** High for at-risk patients

### 4.2 Early Warning System
- Pattern detection for concerning symptom combinations
- Automatic specialist alerts
- Clear escalation pathways
- **Impact:** High - safety critical

### 4.3 Outcome Story Generator
- Anonymised progress patterns
- "People like you typically..." reassurance
- Marketing-ready (ethically anonymised)
- **Impact:** Medium - onboarding and retention

---

## Features NOT on Roadmap

### Nutrition Tracking - Intentionally Excluded
**Reason:** High risk in cancer context
- Can increase overwhelm
- Treatment nutrition is complex
- Risk of promoting unhealthy behaviors

**Alternative:** Light hydration reminders, dietitian referrals

## Success Metrics

### Engagement
- Daily active users
- Workout completion rate
- Streak length averages

### Confidence
- Confidence score trends
- "I Have 3 Minutes" usage
- Safety signal distribution (green vs amber vs red)

### Clinical
- Appointment summary exports
- Specialist intervention rates
- Patient-reported outcomes

### Retention
- 30-day retention rate
- Dropout prediction accuracy
- Reactivation success
